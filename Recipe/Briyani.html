Briyani

Description

Chicken Biryani is a fragrant South Asian dish featuring marinated chicken, aromatic basmati rice, and a blend of spices like saffron, cloves, and cardamom. Cooked using the "dum" method, it results in tender chicken and flavorful rice. Often garnished with fried onions and herbs, it's a festive, flavorful meal.

Ingredients

  • 1.5 pounds (700g) chicken pieces (preferably bone-in)
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • salt to taste
  • 2 cups basmati rice, rinsed and soaked for 30 minutes
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 4 cloves
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 3 tablespoons ghee or vegetable oil
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 green chilies, slit (optional)
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro leaves, chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon saffron threads soaked in 2 tablespoons warm milk (optional)
  • 1/4 cup fried onions (for garnish)
  • 2 boiled eggs (optional, for garnish)
  • Fresh lemon wedges (optional, for serving)
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 4 cloves
  • 3-4 green cardamom pods
  • 1 black cardamom pod
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 star anise
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds

Steps

    Marinate the Chicken:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, ground coriander, and salt. Add the chicken pieces and coat well. Marinate for at least 1 hour, preferably overnight in the refrigerator.

    2. Prepare the Rice:

  2. In a large pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil. Add the bay leaf, cloves, cardamom pods, cinnamon stick, and salt.
  3. Add the soaked and drained rice. Cook until the rice is 70-80% cooked (it should still have a bite to it). Drain the rice and set aside.

    4. Cook the Chicken:

  4. In a large heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven, heat the ghee or oil over medium heat. Add the whole spices (bay leaf, cloves, cardamom pods, black cardamom, cinnamon stick, star anise, and cumin seeds) and saut√© for a minute until fragrant.
  5. Add the sliced onions and cook until golden brown. Add the chopped tomatoes and green chilies, and cook until the tomatoes are soft.
  6. Add the marinated chicken along with the marinade. Cook until the chicken is partially cooked (about 70%).

    7. Layer the Biryani:

  7. Reduce the heat to low. Spread half of the partially cooked rice over the chicken in an even layer.
  8. Sprinkle half of the chopped cilantro and mint leaves over the rice. Add a layer of the remaining rice on top.
  9. Drizzle the saffron milk over the rice, if using. Sprinkle the remaining cilantro and mint leaves over the top. Garnish with fried onions.

    10. Dum Cooking:

  10. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid or seal it with aluminum foil and place the lid on top. Cook on low heat for about 20-25 minutes, allowing the steam to cook the rice and chicken fully.

    11. Serve:

  11. Once cooked, gently fluff the biryani with a fork to mix the layers without breaking the rice grains. Serve hot, garnished with boiled eggs and lemon wedges if desired. Enjoy with raita or a simple salad.
